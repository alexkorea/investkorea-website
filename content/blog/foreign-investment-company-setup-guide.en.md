<h1>Complete Guide to Establishing a Foreign Investment Corporation</h1>

The process of establishing a foreign investment corporation can be complex, but it can proceed smoothly if the correct procedures are followed. Generally, to establish a foreign investment corporation, steps such as submitting an investment plan, registering the corporation, and tax reporting are required, along with necessary documents and conditions for each process.

<h2 id="faq">Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>Q: How much does it cost to establish a foreign investment corporation?</h3>
A: Generally, it costs about KRW 1.5 million to 7 million, including corporation registration and tax reporting fees. The fee is tiered by case complexity (investment structure, regulated industries, source-of-funds verification): simple cases 1.5M–3M, standard 3M–5M, complex (multinational equity, regulated sectors, on-site due diligence) 5M–7M.

<h3>Q: How do I write an investment plan?</h3>
A: The investment plan should include business goals, investment amount, expected profits, etc.

<h3>Q: Are there any special restrictions on establishing a foreign investment corporation?</h3>
A: There may be restrictions depending on the industry, and consultation with the relevant authority is necessary.

<h3>Q: What is the minimum capital required for corporation establishment?</h3>
A: The minimum capital varies by industry, but generally, at least 10 million won is required.

<h3>Q: What additional documents are needed after corporation establishment?</h3>
A: After obtaining the business registration certificate, tax reporting and business-related permits may be required.

<h3>Q: Can foreign shareholders participate in the corporation establishment?</h3>
A: Yes, foreign shareholders can participate in the corporation establishment.

<h3>Q: What procedures are necessary for operation after corporation establishment?</h3>
A: During operation, procedures such as tax reporting and bookkeeping are required.</h3>
