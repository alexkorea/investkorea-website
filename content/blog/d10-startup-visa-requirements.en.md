<h1>Conditions and Procedures for Applying for the Korean Startup Visa (D-10)</h1>

The Korean Startup Visa (D-10) is a visa that supports foreigners in starting business activities in South Korea. To apply for this visa, specific conditions must be met, and the application process is clearly defined. Through the startup visa, foreign entrepreneurs can secure a legal basis to start and operate a business in Korea.

The application conditions are as follows. Generally, foreigners wishing to apply for the startup visa must have a business plan, meet capital requirements, submit a business plan, and have relevant experience. It is important to understand the necessary documents and procedures accurately.

<h2 id="section-conditions">Eligibility for Startup Visa Application</h2>
The eligibility for applying for the Startup Visa (D-10) is as follows. Applicants must be foreigners who meet the legal requirements to operate a business in Korea. Generally, entrepreneurial experience or expertise in a relevant field is required, and the business idea must be suitable for the Korean market.

<h2 id="section-procedure">Application Procedure</h2>
The application procedure for the startup visa proceeds as follows.

1. **Draft Business Plan**: Must include business idea, market analysis, financial plan, etc.
2. **Prepare Required Documents**: Include proof of capital, experience certificates, proof of Korean language proficiency, etc.
3. **Submit Application**: Submit the application to the relevant immigration office.
4. **Interview**: An in-person interview may be required, and there may be questions about the business plan.
5. **Wait for Visa Issuance**: Notification of visa issuance will be provided after review.

<h2 id="section-timeline">Processing Time</h2>
Generally, the processing time for a startup visa application is about 2-3 months. However, it may vary depending on the workload of the relevant authorities, so it is important to plan ahead.

<h2 id="section-cost">Cost</h2>
The cost incurred when applying for the startup visa is generally around 60,000 KRW. Additionally, there may be costs for notarizing required documents, so please keep this in mind.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">Common Mistakes and Warnings</h2>
Common mistakes that occur during the application process include the following.

1. **Incomplete Document Submission**: All documents must be complete.
2. **Unclear Business Plan**: A valid and specific business plan is required.
3. **Insufficient Proof of Capital**: Applications will be rejected if capital requirements are not met.

<h3>Q: How long does it take to issue the visa after applying for the startup visa?</h3>
A: Generally, it takes about 2-3 months.

<h3>Q: What are the capital requirements?</h3>
A: A minimum capital of over 30 million KRW is required.

<h3>Q: How should proof of Korean language proficiency be submitted?</h3>
A: You must submit the TOPIK test score report.

<h3>Q: How should the business plan be written?</h3>
A: It should include the business idea, market analysis, financial plan, etc.

<h3>Q: Is an interview required when applying for the visa?</h3>
A: Yes, an in-person interview may be required.

<h3>Q: Are there any additional costs besides the application fee?</h3>
A: Notarization fees for documents may also be incurred.

<h3>Q: What should I do if my visa application is rejected?</h3>
A: You can check the reason for rejection, make corrections, and reapply.
