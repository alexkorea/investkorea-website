<h1>Conditions and Procedures for Applying for the Korean Startup Visa (D-10)</h1>

The Korean Startup Visa (D-10) is a visa that allows foreigners to stay in Korea for the purpose of starting a business. To apply for this visa, specific conditions must be met, and the application process is clearly defined. Those planning to start a business in Korea through the Startup Visa (D-10) should refer to the information below.

<h2 id="id1">D-10 Visa Application Conditions</h2>
To apply for the Startup Visa (D-10), the following conditions must be met:

1. **Business Plan**: A specific plan for starting a business in Korea is required, along with supporting documentation.
2. **Financial Capability**: Applicants must possess the necessary funds for starting a business, typically requiring a minimum capital of 5,000,000 KRW.
3. **Education and Experience**: Proof of educational qualifications (at least a university degree) or relevant experience (at least 3 years) is required.
4. **Business Idea**: Applicants must have a business idea suitable for the Korean market.

<h2 id="id2">Application Process</h2>
The application process for the D-10 visa is as follows:

1. **Online Application**: Complete the visa application form online through the Korean Immigration Service website.
2. **Document Submission**: Prepare the required documents and submit them to the relevant immigration office.
3. **Review**: A review of the submitted documents will take place.
4. **Visa Issuance**: If the review is passed, the visa will be issued.

<h2 id="id4">Application Fees</h2>
The application fee for the D-10 visa is generally around 60,000 KRW. However, this fee may vary depending on individual circumstances, so it is necessary to check with the relevant authority for the exact cost.

<h2 id="id5">Post-Application Precautions</h2>
After applying for the visa, please be aware of the following:

- Additional documents may be required based on the review results.
- After the visa is issued, the business must be completed within the specified period.
- If a visa extension is needed, it must be applied for at least one month in advance.

<h2 id="id6">FAQ</h2>
<h3>Q: How long is the D-10 visa valid?</h3>
A: The D-10 visa is generally valid for 6 months to 1 year.

<h3>Q: How do I extend my visa?</h3>
A: Visa extensions must be applied for at the immigration office, and necessary documents must be prepared.

<h3>Q: Where can I inquire if I have additional questions about the startup visa?</h3>
A: You can contact the immigration office or a professional administrative agent.

<h3>Q: How much capital is required when applying for the startup visa?</h3>
A: Generally, a minimum of 5,000,000 KRW is required.

<h3>Q: Can I change my business after applying for the startup visa in Korea?</h3>
A: If you change your business, you may need to apply for a new visa, so careful consideration is necessary.

<h3>Q: How long does the review period take after applying for the startup visa?</h3>
A: Typically, it takes about 1 month.

<h3>Q: What should I do if my startup visa application is denied?</h3>
A: After confirming the reason for denial, you can reapply by reflecting the necessary corrections.
